About Me

A story of food, travels, failure, and design

The Story of

An entrepreneur
Raised by two self-made, small-business-owning parents it’s not too surprising why I was printing and selling comic books for a profit in third grade or selling all the books I had already read in a makeshift book store in the back of my mom’s vet clinic when I was 8. I’m an entrepreneur at heart and am always looking for opportunities to fail miserably. Because as every good entrepreneur knows, failure is the first step to success.
An Art Director
My two hard-working, loving parents dished out a pretty penny for my private business school education and it was all going to pay off with a plush financial career until I found a new calling while studying abroad in the financial capital of the world, London. A curious series about some 1960’s ad men soundly and swiftly derailed my dreams of investment banking. Mad Men revealed an avenue where my creativity and business acumen could coexist. I picked up photoshop, read straight through the “Do It Yourself Lobotomy” and secured a dismally low-paying creative internship as quickly as I could. Now I work from concept to execution on various projects involving brands like Butterfinger, Hot Pockets, Lean Pockets, Nestea, Jarritos, Caress, LinkedIn, IZZE and others.
A FOOD EATER
I love food. I love eating it, making it, tasting it, smelling it. I’ve made reservations 2 months in advance for it. I’ve waited in a parking lot for 3 hours for it. I’ve given up a week’s paycheck for a meal-worth of it. I’ve dressed up in a tux fancier than my prom outfit so I could satisfy the dress code to be in the same room with it. I simply love food. My favorites? I love me some southern BBQ, Shabu Shabu, or well-crafted bánh mì. Or if you want to get serious, nothing beats the bastilla at Tagine.
